Pistyll Rhaedar

Britains tallest single-drop waterfall stood at 240ft (80m) A captivating waterfall in the Berwyn Mountains.

Just inside Wales, west of Oswestry and Shrewsbury.



Visiting The Falls

Pistyll Rhaeadar is open to the public all throughout the year. There is no charge to visit the falls. Donations for the upkeep of the site are gratefully received in a box next to the gate.

Prices

Summer

Cars: £5

Motorbikes: £4

Winter

Cars: £4

Motorbikes: £3


There is an overnight charge if cars are left here overnight.

All payments cover conservation projects around the site. If you don't wish to pay, there are roadside parking 100 yards before you reach us.

Walks

A short walk from the car park through the wooden gate next to the tea room, takes you to the base of the waterfall.

At the base of the falls there is an iron bridge crossing the river which takes you to the woodland walks and a public footpath.

The top of the falls is reached via a public footpath which takes around 20 minutes.

Food and Drink

Opening hours : 10am-4pm (winter) 9.30am - 6pm (summer)

Our family-friendly restaurant seats 38 people indoors and we have outdoor bench seating for around 38 people.

We have created an extensive menu to cater for the large range of visitors that visit Pistyll Rhaeadar
The menu includes breakfast, sandwiches, baked potatoes and snacks, as well as main course meals and the chefs special menu, with a dessert menu to follow.

You are responsible for your own and your party's safety.

The falls have not been tamed with concrete, safety railings and warning signs.
